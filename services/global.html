Global Talent Visa UK

The UK Global Talent Visa is an immigration pathway created for highly skilled individuals in fields such as science, research, technology, engineering, and the arts and humanities who wish to live and work in the UK.

Important: This visa is designed for established leaders (Exceptional Talent) and emerging future leaders (Exceptional Promise) who can contribute significantly to their field in the UK.

What is the UK Global Talent Visa?

The Global Talent Visa is a popular immigration route for non-British nationals seeking to work in the UK within specific specialist sectors.

Endorsement Bodies:

In most situations, applicants must first obtain an official endorsement from one of six approved endorsing organisations.

Endorsements for eligible candidates working in science, engineering, medicine, social sciences, humanities, and academic research are issued by the following bodies:

  • The British Academy
  • The Royal Academy of Engineering
  • The Royal Society
  • UK Research and Innovation (UKRI)

When applying for endorsement, academics and researchers may qualify under four routes: academic or research appointments, individual fellowships, endorsed funders, and peer review.

For applicants in non-academic arts, culture, and digital technology, endorsements are managed by Arts Council England.

Two-Stage Process: Securing an endorsement from an approved body is the initial stage of the process. Once obtained, the applicant must submit both the endorsement and visa application to the Home Office for approval.

Who is Eligible for Global Talent Visa in UK?

This visa is available to applicants aged 18 or over who demonstrate either exceptional talent (established leaders) or exceptional promise (emerging future leaders) in the following areas:

Academia or Research

Science, engineering, humanities, social sciences, medicine

Arts and Culture

Architecture, fashion, film, television, performing arts

Digital Technology

Tech leadership, product development, digital innovation

Eligibility Criteria: Eligibility is based on endorsement or receipt of a qualifying award rather than a points-based system, through one of the following routes:

  • Holding a prize listed under the Prestigious Prizes category in the immigration rules (for example, a Nobel Prize or Golden Global Award)
  • Submitting an initial application with a valid endorsement issued by an approved body within the previous three months, provided the endorsement has not been withdrawn
  • Applying for an extension and demonstrating compliance with the earnings requirement, with confirmation that any previously issued endorsement remains valid

Application Process: The application process can be demanding, as both the endorsement and visa stages involve strict assessment criteria. While the Global Talent immigration rules provide detailed eligibility guidance, applicants are encouraged to seek advice from an immigration solicitor if there is any uncertainty about their circumstances.

Applications can be made from outside the UK or by switching from certain eligible UK visas, such as the Skilled Worker or Innovator visa.

Endorsement for the Global Talent Visa

Applicants who believe they are eligible should carefully review the requirements of each endorsing body and clearly demonstrate how they meet the criteria.

Endorsing Bodies and Their Specializations:

Arts Council England

Arts, culture, film, music, dance, fashion, architecture

The Royal Society

Natural sciences, mathematics, engineering, medical research

Royal Academy of Engineering

Engineering, technology, innovation

British Academy

Humanities, social sciences

UK Research and Innovation

Research across all disciplines

Tech Nation

Digital technology sector

Process: After submitting an endorsement application and paying the relevant fee, applicants will receive a decision. If successful, they may then proceed with their visa application to the Home Office.

Eligibility Requirements Per Endorsement or Field

All applicants must be at least 18 years old and meet the criteria specific to their chosen field. Below are examples of field-based requirements by endorsing body.

Royal Society, British Academy, and UK Research and Innovation (UKRI)

Applicants in natural and medical sciences—such as biology, chemistry, engineering, mathematics, and physics—typically apply through one of the following organisations:

  • The Royal Society of England
  • UK Research and Innovation (UKRI)
  • The British Academy
Academic or Research Appointment

Applicants holding a research role with an eligible UK organisation must:

  • Be responsible for academic, research, or innovation leadership
  • Lead or direct an individual or group research or innovation project
Individual Fellowships

Applicants must have been awarded an approved individual fellowship by the British Academy, Royal Academy of Engineering, or Royal Society.

Endorsed Funders

Applicants funded by an approved organisation must:

  • Hold a successful grant worth at least £30,000 from a UKRI-approved funder, covering a minimum of two years
  • Spend at least 50% of their time fulfilling the grant conditions
Peer Review Applicants

Applicants must:

  • Be actively engaged in research at a recognised institution or within industry
  • Hold a PhD or equivalent research experience, including clinical or industrial research
  • Be at an early career stage if applying under Exceptional Promise

Arts Council England

Arts Council England assesses applications from professional artists and practitioners, including those working in film, music, dance, fashion, architecture, and related fields.

To qualify, applicants must:

  • Have produced high-quality, professionally published work within the past five years
  • Show consistent and recent professional activity during this period
  • Demonstrate international recognition and demand in more than one country for Exceptional Talent applications
  • Show a developing track record and international demand in at least one country for Exceptional Promise applications

Tech Nation

Tech Nation oversees endorsements for applicants in the digital technology sector. Applicants must demonstrate:

  • Recognition as a leader or emerging leader in digital technology within the past five years
  • Evidence of founding or holding a senior role in a product-led digital business, or contributing to a new digital concept
  • Work that advances the digital technology field
  • Academic contributions through published, peer-endorsed research

Applying for an Endorsement

Below is a general overview of the endorsement process.

1

Eligibility Assessment

Applicants must confirm that they meet the Global Talent criteria and are recognised as leaders or emerging leaders in their field.

2

Choose the Right Endorsement Route

Selecting the correct category is critical. Exceptional Talent is for established professionals with proven achievements and global recognition, while Exceptional Promise is for early-career professionals with strong potential and international impact.

3

Gather Required Evidence

Applicants must submit clear and legible supporting documents in PDF or image format. These may include:

  • A full job description
  • A CV
  • Grant award letters
  • HR confirmation letters
  • Letters of recommendation
  • Evidence demonstrating exceptional talent or promise
4

Submit Endorsement Application

The endorsement application (Stage 1) is submitted online via the Home Office website. This includes personal details, qualifications, fellowships, publications, awards, and patents.

5

Attend an Endorsement Review

The endorsing body will assess the application and either approve it or provide feedback.

6

Await Decision and Next Steps

Decisions are usually made within 2–8 weeks. Successful applicants receive an endorsement letter valid for three months, during which they must apply for the visa or submit both applications simultaneously.

Fast-Track Application for Prestigious Award

Applicants holding an eligible prestigious award may bypass the endorsement stage and apply directly for the Global Talent Visa.

How to Apply for the UK Global Talent Visa?

After receiving endorsement, or at the same time, applicants can proceed with the visa application.

1

Complete the Online Application

Applicants must complete the online form via the UK government website and pay the required fee.

2

Attend your Biometric Appointment

Some applicants will need to attend a visa application centre to provide fingerprints and a photograph.

3

Submit Supporting Documents

Applicants must submit their endorsement letter and supporting documents to UK Visas and Immigration. Professional legal advice is strongly recommended to ensure the application is accurate and complete.

4

Pay the Immigration Health Surcharge

Applicants must pay the Immigration Health Surcharge to access NHS services.

5

Attend an Interview (if required)

An interview may be requested in some cases.

6

Wait for a Decision

Processing times vary, and average timelines can be checked on the UK government website.

7

Receive your visa

Successful applicants will receive their immigration status in digital form.

Benefits of the Global Talent Visa UK

The Global Talent Visa offers significant advantages over other UK work visas.

Work Flexibility

Visa holders may work for multiple employers or operate their own business within their area of expertise. Job changes do not need to be reported, and no sponsorship is required. The only restriction is employment as a professional sportsperson or coach.

No Sponsorship Required

Employers do not need a sponsor licence, and applicants do not require a certificate of sponsorship.

Fast-Track Path to Settlement

Exceptional Talent holders can apply for settlement after three years, while Exceptional Promise holders may apply after five years.

Bring Dependants

Visa holders may bring their partner and children under 18, who can live, work, and study in the UK.

What documents do you need to apply for the Global Talent Visa?

Document requirements depend on the applicant's field and endorsing body, but may include:

Essential Documents:
  • CVs and recommendation letters
  • Evidence of professional achievements
  • Business or research documentation
  • Grant confirmations
  • Passports and TB test results (if applicable)
  • Certified translations of non-English documents

Waiting Time and Global Talent Visa Fees

Waiting Time for Global Talent Visa Application

Most applications are decided within three weeks outside the UK and eight weeks within the UK. Additional information requests may extend processing times. Applications can be submitted up to three months before the intended travel date, with priority services available for an additional fee.

Global Talent Visa Fees

  • Endorsement-based applications: £561 for endorsement + £766 for visa = £1,327 total
  • Prestigious award applications: £205 total (no endorsement required)
  • Visa fee only: £205
  • Dependants: £766 each + Immigration Health Surcharge

Dependants on UK Global Talent Visa

Eligible dependants include spouses, partners, and children. Dependants can work, study, and apply for settlement when eligible.

Extend the Global Talent Visa in UK

Visas can be extended for up to five years if endorsement or award status remains valid and earnings in the expert field can be demonstrated. Applications must be made before the current visa expires and cost £205.

Switch to the Global Talent Visa

Applicants already in the UK may be able to switch, provided they meet eligibility requirements. Switching is not permitted from certain visa categories, including visit visas and short-term student visas. Switching costs £205.

Settle in the UK with Global Talent Visa

Applicants may qualify for indefinite leave to remain after three or five years, depending on endorsement type:

Settlement Timelines:
  • Exceptional Talent: Eligible for ILR after 3 years
  • Exceptional Promise: Eligible for ILR after 5 years

Time spent on other eligible visas may be combined. Applicants must meet residence, English language, and Life in the UK test requirements. The ILR application fee is £3,029.

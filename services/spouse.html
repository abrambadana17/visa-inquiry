UK Spouse Visa Overview

The UK Spouse Visa is available to individuals who are legally married to a British or Irish citizen, a person who is settled in the United Kingdom (holding Indefinite Leave to Remain or settled status), an individual with pre-settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme, a holder of a Turkish Businessperson or Turkish Worker visa, or a person granted refugee status or humanitarian protection in the UK.

This visa permits the applicant to enter or remain in the UK in order to live together with their spouse on a long-term basis. An application may also be submitted where the sponsoring spouse is currently outside the UK but intends to return and establish residence in the UK with the applicant.

Settlement Route: The UK Spouse Visa forms part of a settlement route, enabling eligible applicants to apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) after completing five years of continuous residence in the UK, provided that all applicable Immigration Rules continue to be satisfied throughout the duration of the route.

1. UK Spouse Visa Eligibility and Core Requirements

To qualify for a UK Spouse Visa, applicants must demonstrate to UK Visas and Immigration that all relevant eligibility requirements are met. These include:

  • The sponsoring partner is a British or Irish citizen, settled in the UK, holds pre-settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme, has limited leave as a Turkish Businessperson or Worker, or has refugee status or humanitarian protection in the UK
  • Both partners are aged 18 or over
  • The couple is not related within a prohibited degree of relationship
  • The partners have met in person
  • The marriage is legally valid and recognised under UK law
  • The relationship is genuine and subsisting
  • There is a clear intention to live together permanently in the UK
  • Any previous relationships have permanently ended
  • The financial requirement is satisfied
  • Adequate accommodation is available
  • The applicant meets the English language requirement

Professional Advice: The precise requirements may differ depending on individual circumstances. Professional legal advice is often advisable to ensure full compliance with Immigration Rules and Home Office guidance.

2. Who Can Sponsor a UK Spouse Visa Application?

A Spouse Visa application must be supported by a qualifying sponsor who falls within one of the following categories:

  • A British or Irish citizen residing in the UK or intending to return with the applicant
  • A person holding Indefinite Leave to Remain or settled status under the EU Settlement Scheme
  • An individual with pre-settled status under Appendix EU
  • A Turkish Businessperson or Worker under Appendix ECAA
  • A person granted refugee status or humanitarian protection in the UK

Sponsors Outside UK: Sponsors may be outside the UK at the time of application, provided there is a genuine intention to return and live permanently with the applicant in the UK.

3. Minimum Age Requirement for a UK Spouse Visa

Both the applicant and the sponsoring partner must be at least 18 years old on the date the Spouse Visa application is submitted.

4. Demonstrating a Genuine Relationship for a UK Spouse Visa

The relationship requirement comprises several elements:

  • The couple must not fall within a prohibited family relationship
  • The partners must have met in person
  • The marriage must be legally valid
  • The relationship must be genuine and ongoing
  • There must be an intention to live together permanently in the UK
  • Any prior relationships must have ended permanently

Each of these elements must be evidenced to the satisfaction of the Home Office.

5. Restrictions on Family Relationships Under Spouse Visa Rules

UK immigration law prohibits spouses from being related within specific degrees, as defined by the Marriage Act 1949 and related legislation. Relationships that fall within these prohibited categories are not eligible for a Spouse Visa.

Extended Family Relationships: Certain extended family relationships may only qualify if both parties are aged 21 or over and additional conditions are met. The Home Office applies these rules strictly.

6. UK Spouse Visa: Face-to-Face Meeting Requirement

Applicants must show that they and their spouse have met face-to-face and formed a mutual acquaintance. Mere virtual contact or brief encounters without meaningful interaction do not satisfy this requirement.

7. Marriage Recognition and Validity for UK Spouse Visa Applications

To qualify for a UK Spouse Visa, the marriage must be legally valid and recognised under UK law.

Marriages in the UK

Must comply with statutory formalities, including proper notice, authorised venues, and lawful officiation.

Overseas marriages

Are recognised if they were lawfully conducted, legally valid in the country of marriage, free from legal impediments, and any prior marriages were legally terminated.

Important: Official marriage certificates or recognised equivalents must be provided.

8. How to Prove a Genuine and Subsisting Relationship for a Spouse Visa

Applicants must demonstrate that their relationship is genuine and ongoing at all stages of the visa route. The Home Office assesses this on a case-by-case basis using a broad range of evidence, including:

  • Cohabitation history
  • Shared financial responsibilities
  • Children and parental responsibilities
  • Communication and travel history
  • Future plans as a couple

Evidence Requirement: No single document is sufficient. A consistent body of credible evidence is required.

9. UK Spouse Visa: Proving Intention to Live Together in the UK

Applicants must prove a shared intention to live together on a permanent basis in the UK. Temporary absences are permitted, provided they are consistent with maintaining family life in the UK and do not indicate habitual residence elsewhere.

10. Evidence That Previous Relationships Have Ended for Spouse Visa Applications

Where either partner has been previously married, documentary proof of legal termination—such as a decree absolute or overseas equivalent—must be submitted. An undissolved marriage will prevent approval under the Spouse Visa route.

11. Meeting the Financial Requirement for a UK Spouse Visa

Applicants must meet the financial requirement to demonstrate that they can be maintained without public funds. The standard minimum income threshold is £29,000 gross per year, unless transitional provisions apply.

Permitted sources include:

  • Employment income
  • Self-employment or company director income
  • Non-employment income
  • Cash savings over £16,000
  • Pension income

Important: Strict evidential rules apply, and failure to comply is a common reason for refusal.

12. Accommodation Requirements for UK Spouse Visa Applicants

Applicants must show that suitable accommodation is available in the UK without reliance on public funds. The accommodation must not be overcrowded and must comply with public health regulations.

13. UK Spouse Visa English Language Requirement: Tests and Exemptions

Applicants must meet specified English language standards:

  • A1 for initial applications
  • A2 for extensions
  • B1 for settlement

Exemptions: Apply in limited circumstances, including age, medical conditions, or nationality.

14. UK Spouse Visa Exceptions: Human Rights and Exceptional Circumstances

Applicants who cannot meet all standard requirements may still qualify where refusal would breach Article 8 of the European Convention on Human Rights. Such cases are assessed under the exceptional circumstances provisions of Appendix FM.

15. Applying for a Spouse Visa Inside or Outside the UK

Applications must generally be made from outside the UK unless the applicant holds a visa allowing a stay of more than six months. Visitor visa holders are not permitted to switch from within the UK.

16. Switching to a UK Spouse Visa From Another Immigration Route

Applicants may be able to switch to the Spouse Visa route from eligible categories such as Skilled Worker, Student, Graduate, or Fiancé(e) visas, provided all requirements are met.

17. How to Include Children in a UK Spouse Visa Application

Eligible dependent children under 18 may apply alongside or separately from the main applicant, subject to accommodation and financial requirements.

18. UK Spouse Visa Application Process: Online Form and Supporting Documents

Applications must be submitted online using the correct form, with supporting documents prepared in accordance with Home Office requirements. Errors or omissions can result in refusal.

19. Checklist of Documents for a UK Spouse Visa Application

Documentary requirements vary by case. Incorrect formatting, missing documents, or failure to meet evidential rules frequently lead to refusal. Tailored legal advice is strongly recommended.

20. Home Office Fees for UK Spouse Visa Applications

  • Applications from outside the UK: £1,938
  • Applications from within the UK: £1,321

Additional Charges: Apply for priority services.

21. Spouse Visa UK Processing Times and Decision Waiting Times

  • Outside the UK: Approximately 12 weeks (30 working days with Priority)
  • Inside the UK: Approximately 8 weeks, or longer in complex cases

Super Priority Service: Available for in-country applications only.

22. How Long Is a UK Spouse Visa Valid For?

Initial grants are issued for 33 months (overseas) or 30 months (in-country). Extensions are granted for a further 30 months. Settlement is available after five years.

23. Can Spouse Visa Holders Work in the UK?

Employment Rights: Spouse Visa holders have unrestricted permission to work in the UK.

24. Reasons Why UK Spouse Visa Applications Are Refused and How to Avoid Them

Refusals often arise due to:

  • Insufficient relationship evidence
  • Failure to meet the financial requirement
  • Incorrect documentation
  • Invalid or unrecognised marriages
  • English language non-compliance

Preparation is Key: Careful preparation significantly reduces refusal risk.

25. Spouse Visa Refused: Appeal and Reapplication Options

Most refusals carry a right of appeal on human rights grounds. Appeals can be lengthy and complex, and specialist legal representation is advisable.

26. How to Apply for ILR After Five Years on a UK Spouse Visa

Applicants may apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain after completing five years, subject to residence, English language (B1), and Life in the UK Test requirements.

27. When Can Spouse Visa Holders Apply for a UK Passport?

Following ILR, spouses of British citizens may apply for naturalisation immediately. Others must wait 12 months.

28. UK Spouse Visa to British Citizenship: Application Timeline Explained

  • Initial Spouse Visa
  • Extension
  • Indefinite Leave to Remain
  • British Citizenship

Each stage carries distinct requirements and evidential standards.

29. Marriage in the UK: Selecting the Appropriate Visa Route

Those intending to marry in the UK must choose between a Marriage Visitor Visa (no settlement) or a Fiancé(e) Visa, which allows switching to the Spouse Visa after marriage.

30. How Our Immigration Barristers Can Help

Spouse Visa applications are complex and highly evidence-driven. Specialist immigration barristers provide strategic advice, document preparation, representation in appeals, and support through extensions and settlement applications, ensuring compliance and maximising prospects of success.

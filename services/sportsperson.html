International Sportsperson Visa UK

Elite athletes and professional coaches from overseas can live and work in the UK under the International Sportsperson visa.

Important: This visa is designed for high-level international athletes and coaches who can contribute to the development of their sport at the highest level in the UK.

What is the International Sportsperson Visa?

The International Sportsperson visa is a long-term UK work visa designed for high-level international athletes and coaches. It allows them to enter the UK to perform and develop their sport at an elite level.

This visa has defined eligibility conditions that applicants must meet. It can be granted for up to three years initially and may be extended. After five continuous years in the UK under this route, applicants may be eligible to apply for indefinite leave to remain.

Key Features:

  • Long-term work visa for elite athletes and coaches
  • Initial validity up to 3 years
  • Extendable and leads to settlement
  • Requires endorsement from Sports Governing Body
  • Allows family members to accompany

What Can I Do With the International Sportsperson Visa?

This visa allows you to:

  • Work for your sponsoring organisation in the role stated on your Certificate of Sponsorship
  • Take on a second job in limited circumstances
  • Represent your national team while in the UK
  • Work as a sports broadcaster
  • Carry out voluntary work
  • Study
  • Bring your partner and dependent children to the UK

What You Cannot Do With the International Sportsperson Visa

You are not permitted to:

  • Access public funds
  • Set up or operate a business
  • Start a second job before beginning your main sponsored role

Note: Violating these conditions may result in visa cancellation and affect future immigration applications.

What Are the Eligibility Criteria for International Sportsperson Visa?

To qualify for this visa, you must:

  • Receive an endorsement from a Home Office-approved Sports Governing Body confirming that:
    • You are an elite sportsperson or a suitably qualified coach
    • You are internationally recognised in your sport
    • Your employment will contribute to the development of your sport in the UK at the highest level
  • Hold a valid Certificate of Sponsorship
  • Meet the English language requirement if staying longer than 12 months
  • Have sufficient funds to support yourself in the UK
  • Provide details of your travel history over the past five years
  • Submit tuberculosis test results if required
  • Be offered employment that meets UK minimum wage and employment law requirements

Important: The endorsement from a Sports Governing Body is crucial and must demonstrate your elite status and contribution to UK sport development.

Eligibility for Emerging Professional Footballers

Updated Home Office guidance introduced the Elite Significant Contribution (ESC) route, allowing certain young footballers who show exceptional potential to qualify even if they are not yet internationally established.

ESC Route Quotas:

  • Premier League and Championship clubs: May sponsor up to 4 players per season
  • League One and League Two clubs: May sponsor up to 2 players per season

Eligibility is assessed based on the standard of leagues previously played in and the FIFA ranking of the country represented.

This special route recognizes that exceptional young talent may not yet have established international recognition but shows clear potential to contribute significantly to UK football.

How Much Money Do I Need for the International Sportsperson Visa?

Applicants must usually show at least £1,270 in personal savings, held for a minimum of 28 days within the 31 days before applying.

Financial Exemptions:

This requirement does not apply if:

  • You have already lived in the UK for at least 12 months
  • Your sponsor agrees to cover your initial living costs, as confirmed on your Certificate of Sponsorship

What Are the English Language Requirements?

If you intend to stay in the UK for more than 12 months, you must demonstrate English language ability. This can be done by:

  • Passing an approved Secure English Language Test at CEFR level A1 or higher
  • Holding a UK academic qualification
  • Holding an overseas degree taught in English and confirmed as equivalent to a UK degree by ECCTIS
  • Being a citizen of a majority English-speaking country

Note: For stays of 12 months or less, the English language requirement does not apply.

How to Apply for International Sportsperson Visa?

Before applying, you must obtain an endorsement from your sport's governing body, followed by a Certificate of Sponsorship. You must apply within three months of receiving the certificate.

Application Process:

  1. Obtain Endorsement: Secure endorsement from the relevant Sports Governing Body
  2. Certificate of Sponsorship: Obtain valid CoS from your UK sponsor
  3. Gather Documents: Prepare all required supporting documents
  4. Complete Online Application: Submit application on gov.uk within 3 months of CoS
  5. Biometric Information: Provide fingerprints and photograph at visa application centre or via UK Immigration: ID Check app
  6. Submit Documents: Provide all required supporting evidence
  7. Pay Fees: Pay application fee and immigration health surcharge
  8. Await Decision: Wait for processing and decision notification

Timing: Apply within 3 months of receiving your Certificate of Sponsorship to ensure validity.

What Supporting Documents Do I Need to Provide?

You will typically need to submit:

  • Your Certificate of Sponsorship reference number
  • Proof of English language ability, if applicable
  • Evidence of sufficient funds, unless exempt
  • A valid passport or acceptable ID
  • Previous passports or travel documents
  • Tuberculosis test results, if required
  • Your endorsement letter from the sports governing body
  • Evidence of professional achievements and recognition
  • Employment contract or offer letter

Important: Documents not in English or Welsh must be accompanied by certified translations.

International Sportsperson Visa Fees and Processing Time

Application Fees:

  • Stays up to 12 months: £319
  • Stays longer than 12 months (outside UK): £769
  • Stays longer than 12 months (inside UK): £885
  • Immigration Health Surcharge: £1,035 per year

Processing Times:

  • Applications from outside UK: Usually up to 3 weeks
  • Applications from inside UK: Usually up to 8 weeks

Note: Processing times may vary depending on individual circumstances and application volume. Priority services may be available for faster processing.

Extending the International Sportsperson Visa in UK

If your initial visa period is ending, you may apply for an extension provided you:

  • Obtain a renewed endorsement from your sport's governing body
  • Receive a new Certificate of Sponsorship
  • Continue to meet all original eligibility requirements
  • Apply before your current visa expires

The extension fee is £885, and a successful application can grant up to a further three years in the UK.

How can I apply for indefinite leave to remain?

After five years on the International Sportsperson visa, you may qualify for indefinite leave to remain. To be eligible, you must:

  • Have spent no more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month period
  • Receive confirmation from your employer that your role will continue
  • Pass the Life in the UK Test
  • Meet English language requirements at B1 level or higher
  • Have no serious criminal convictions

Settlement Pathway:

The ILR application fee is £3,029. Successful applicants gain permanent residence in the UK with no time restrictions on stay.

What are the requirements for second jobs?

You may take on additional work if:

  • You work no more than 20 hours per week in the second role
  • The role is either in the same profession or listed on the immigration salary list

If these conditions are not met, you must apply for a new visa supported by a new Certificate of Sponsorship.

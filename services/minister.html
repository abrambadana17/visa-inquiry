Minister of Religion Visa UK

The UK Minister of Religion Visa allows ministers and religious workers in pastoral roles to live and work in the UK for up to three years and one month, with the option to extend for a further three years. This visa can also lead to settlement and, eventually, British citizenship.

Important: This visa is designed for religious leaders and pastoral workers who have been offered a role by a UK religious organization that holds a valid sponsor licence.

What is a Minister of Religion Visa in UK?

If you have been offered a role by a religious organisation or institution in the UK, you may apply for a Minister of Religion Visa.

The T2 Minister of Religion Visa enables overseas nationals to enter and reside in the UK to carry out duties such as those of a minister, missionary, or religious worker. Once approved, the visa is granted for a period of three years and one month.

Key Responsibilities Allowed:

  • Leading worship services
  • Delivering sermons and religious teachings
  • Officiating weddings and funerals
  • Organising missionary activities
  • Translating religious texts
  • Coordinating volunteers and community outreach

After five years of lawful residence in the UK under this route, you may become eligible to apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR).

Note: If your role mainly involves non-pastoral duties, such as administrative or clerical work within a faith-based organisation, the UK Religious Worker Visa may be more appropriate.

What Can You Do on a UK Minister of Religion Visa?

With a Minister of Religion Visa, you are permitted to:

  • Live in the UK for up to three years and one month
  • Work for your sponsor in the role specified on your Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)
  • Undertake voluntary work
  • Take a second job in limited circumstances
  • Study, provided it does not interfere with your main role
  • Travel outside the UK and return
  • Bring eligible family members to live with you

Dependants may include your partner and children under 18, as well as eligible children over 18. Each dependant must submit a separate visa application.

What can't you do on a Minister of Religion Visa?

As a Minister of Religion Visa holder, you are not allowed to:

  • Access public funds (benefits)
  • Own more than 10% of your sponsor's shares, unless your annual income exceeds £159,600
  • Work for any employer other than your sponsor (except for limited second job arrangements)
  • Engage in business activities unrelated to your religious role

Important: Violating these conditions may result in visa cancellation and affect future UK immigration applications.

How much is the Minister of Religion Visa Cost?

Application Fees:

  • Applying from outside UK: £769
  • Extending or switching from within UK: £885
  • Dependants: Same fees apply (must apply separately)
  • Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS): £1,035 per year for full visa duration

Applicants must also pay the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS), which is £1,035 per year for the full duration of the visa. Sponsors may cover this cost, provided it is clearly stated in the sponsorship or employment agreement.

Minister of Religion Visa Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for a Minister of Religion Visa, you must:

  • Be at least 18 years old
  • Have a valid Certificate of Sponsorship from a licensed UK sponsor
  • Have a confirmed job offer as a Minister of Religion from an organisation that holds the appropriate sponsor licence
  • Demonstrate sufficient funds to support yourself while in the UK
  • Meet English language requirements (CEFR level B2)
  • Provide details of your travel history
  • Submit tuberculosis test results if required (depending on country of residence)
  • Have a valid passport or travel document

Alternative Route: Those who are not acting as religious leaders but wish to work within a religious organisation may instead consider the Religious Worker Visa.

Documents Required for Minister of Religion Visa

When submitting your application, you must provide:

  • Your Certificate of Sponsorship reference number
  • Evidence of your English language ability
  • Proof of sufficient funds (unless exempt)
  • A valid passport or identity document
  • Expired passports to show your travel history
  • Tuberculosis test results if required
  • Proof of relationship for any dependants
  • Evidence of qualifications or religious training if applicable

Important: Any documents not in English or Welsh must be accompanied by a certified translation. Additional documents may be requested depending on your circumstances.

Minister of Religion Visa Financial Requirements

You must normally show that you have at least £1,270 in personal savings, held for a minimum of 28 consecutive days, with the final day falling within 31 days of your application date.

Financial Exemptions:

You may be exempt from this requirement if:

  • You have lived in the UK for at least 12 months on a valid visa
  • Your sponsor agrees to cover your initial living costs, up to £1,270

Dependants must also meet separate financial requirements. Each dependant needs to show £285 for a partner, £315 for one child, or £200 for each additional child.

Minister of Religion Visa Application Process

Most applications are processed within three weeks. You may apply up to three months before your intended start date.

Application Steps:

  1. Obtain Certificate of Sponsorship: Your UK sponsor must issue you a valid CoS
  2. Prepare Documents: Gather all required supporting evidence
  3. Complete Online Application: Submit application on gov.uk website
  4. Pay Fees: Pay application fee and immigration health surcharge
  5. Biometric Appointment: Attend visa application centre for fingerprints and photo
  6. Submit Documents: Provide all required evidence
  7. Await Decision: Processing typically takes up to 3 weeks
  8. Receive Decision: Notification by email and passport return

Priority services may be available depending on your location, allowing for faster decisions at an additional cost.

Criteria for English Language Proficiency for Minister of Religion Visa

Applicants must meet the English language requirement by demonstrating proficiency at CEFR level B2 through one of the following methods:

  • Passing an approved Secure English Language Test (SELT)
  • Holding a degree taught in English (verified by ECCTIS)
  • Being a national of an exempt English-speaking country
  • Having previously met the requirement in a previous UK visa application

Accepted Tests: IELTS for UKVI, Trinity College London SELT, LanguageCert, or Pearson PTE Academic UKVI at CEFR level B2.

Visa Eligibility for Dependents of a Minister of Religion

You may bring your partner and dependent children to the UK. Each dependant must apply separately and pay the relevant fees.

Eligible Dependants:

  • Spouse or civil partner
  • Unmarried partner (must have lived together for at least 2 years)
  • Children under 18
  • Children over 18 if they are currently in the UK as your dependant

Their visas will usually expire on the same date as yours and they will have similar rights to work and study in the UK.

Requirements for Employers to Sponsor a Minister of Religion

UK employers must hold a valid sponsor licence and meet all sponsorship obligations, including:

  • Issuing a valid Certificate of Sponsorship
  • Offering fair pay and conditions that meet UK minimum wage requirements
  • Keeping accurate records of sponsored workers
  • Ensuring compliance with immigration rules
  • Reporting changes in circumstances to UKVI
  • Cooperating with UKVI compliance visits
  • Paying the appropriate salary as per the going rate for the role

Employers may wish to seek professional legal advice to ensure full compliance with sponsorship duties and avoid licence revocation.

Can I Extend my Minister of Religion Visa in UK?

You may apply to extend your visa for up to a further three years, provided you:

  • Remain in a qualifying role with the same or new sponsor
  • Continue to meet the original eligibility requirements
  • Apply before your current permission expires
  • Have a new Certificate of Sponsorship
  • Meet any updated English language requirements if applicable

Applications must be submitted before your current permission expires, and dependants must also renew their visas at the same time. The extension fee is £885 when applying from within the UK.

Obtaining ILR via the Minister of Religion Visa

After completing five years of continuous residence in the UK on a qualifying visa, you may apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain. To qualify, you must:

  • Have spent no more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month period during the 5-year qualifying period
  • Pass the Life in the UK Test
  • Satisfy the English language criteria at B1 level or higher
  • Remain employed in an eligible role
  • Have no serious criminal convictions
  • Meet the financial requirements
  • Receive confirmation from your employer that your role will continue

Settlement Benefits:

ILR provides permanent residence in the UK with no time restrictions on stay, access to public funds (subject to eligibility), and the ability to apply for British citizenship after 12 months.

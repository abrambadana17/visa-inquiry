UK Unmarried Partner Visa Overview

The UK Unmarried Partner Visa is designed for long-term partners of British or Irish citizens, individuals who are settled in the UK, holders of pre-settled status, Turkish Businessperson or Turkish Worker visa holders, and individuals granted refugee status or humanitarian protection.

This visa allows eligible applicants to join or remain with their unmarried partner in the United Kingdom. Applications may also be made where the sponsoring partner is temporarily outside the UK but intends to return and reside in the UK with the applicant.

Settlement Route: A successful UK Unmarried Partner Visa application places the applicant on a route to settlement, with eligibility to apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR) after completing five years in this category.

1. Unmarried Partner Visa Requirements

To qualify for a UK Unmarried Partner Visa, applicants must satisfy UK Visas and Immigration that all relevant eligibility criteria are met. These requirements include:

  • The sponsoring partner holds qualifying immigration status
  • Both partners are aged 18 or over
  • The partners have met in person
  • The relationship has existed for at least 2 years (or exceptional circumstances apply)
  • The relationship is genuine and subsisting
  • There is a clear intention to live together permanently in the UK
  • Any previous relationships have permanently ended
  • The financial requirement is satisfied
  • Adequate accommodation is available
  • The applicant meets the English language requirement

Professional Advice: The precise requirements applicable may differ depending on individual circumstances. Professional legal advice may be beneficial to ensure full compliance with the Immigration Rules.

2. Status of Sponsoring Partner

To sponsor an Unmarried Partner Visa application, the partner must hold one of the qualifying immigration statuses:

  • British or Irish citizenship
  • Settled status or indefinite leave to remain in the UK
  • Pre-settled status under Appendix EU
  • Limited leave under the Turkish Businessperson or Turkish Worker routes
  • Refugee leave or humanitarian protection in the UK

Sponsors Outside UK: A British citizen sponsor may include an individual accompanying the applicant to the UK. Similarly, a sponsor being granted settlement at the same time as the applicant is also considered eligible.

3. Minimum Age for an Unmarried Partner Visa

Both the applicant and the sponsoring partner must be at least 18 years of age on the date the visa application is submitted.

4. UK Unmarried Partner Visa Relationship Requirement

Applicants must demonstrate that their relationship meets several specific criteria:

  • Evidence of a personal meeting between partners
  • A long-term relationship comparable to marriage or civil partnership
  • The authenticity and continuity of the relationship
  • An intention to live together permanently in the UK
  • Confirmation that any previous relationships have permanently ended

Each element must be substantiated with appropriate documentary evidence.

5. Requirement to Have Met in Person

Applicants must provide evidence that they have met their partner in person. This requires proof of a genuine face-to-face meeting that established a personal relationship.

Important: Brief or incidental encounters followed solely by remote communication are insufficient to meet this requirement. The Home Office looks for evidence of meaningful personal interaction.

6. Requirement to Have Been in a Relationship for at Least 2 Years

Applicants are required to show that they have been in a relationship similar to marriage or civil partnership for a minimum of two years before applying.

Cohabitation Rules: While previous rules required cohabitation, this is no longer mandatory. However, documentary evidence demonstrating the longevity and seriousness of the relationship remains essential.

Exceptional Circumstances: Where the relationship has existed for less than two years, an application may still be considered if exceptional circumstances can be demonstrated. This requires strong supporting evidence.

7. Navigating the New UK Unmarried Partner Visa Rules

Recent changes to the Immigration Rules have altered how certain requirements are assessed, particularly regarding cohabitation and exceptional circumstances.

Key Changes: The removal of mandatory cohabitation requirements means applicants can now demonstrate their relationship through various forms of evidence beyond shared address documentation. However, careful navigation of these updated provisions is essential to ensure compliance.

8. Genuine and Subsisting Relationship Requirement

Applicants must provide compelling evidence that their relationship is genuine and ongoing. Each application is assessed individually, with the Home Office considering all relevant documentation and personal circumstances.

Evidence Strategy: A successful application typically includes a combination of communication records, photographs, travel documents, financial interdependence, and statements from friends and family.

9. Factors Considered by the Home Office When Deciding Whether a Relationship Is Genuine and Subsisting

The Home Office evaluates various factors when assessing relationship authenticity:

  • The length and continuity of the relationship
  • Living arrangements and time spent together
  • Shared financial responsibilities and joint assets
  • Children and parental responsibilities
  • Family connections and social recognition
  • Future plans and commitment in the UK
  • Overall credibility and consistency of evidence submitted

Verification: Where doubts arise, the Home Office may conduct further checks, interviews, or home visits to verify the authenticity of the relationship.

10. Documents Required to Prove a Genuine and Subsisting Relationship

Applicants are expected to submit substantial evidence demonstrating emotional commitment, regular communication, shared responsibilities, and mutual support. Recommended documents include:

Communication Evidence:

  • Call logs, text messages, and chat histories
  • Email correspondence spanning the relationship
  • Social media interactions

Time Together Evidence:

  • Travel tickets and boarding passes
  • Hotel bookings and receipts
  • Photographs together at different times and locations

Financial Interdependence:

  • Joint bank accounts or financial arrangements
  • Evidence of money transfers between partners
  • Shared bills or financial responsibilities

Social Recognition:

  • Statements from friends and family
  • Evidence of celebrating milestones together
  • Joint memberships or activities

Document Quality: All documents should be recent, credible, and sourced from a variety of official or reliable origins. Failure to adequately demonstrate a genuine and subsisting relationship may result in refusal.

11. What is a Genuine and Subsisting Relationship?

A genuine and subsisting relationship is one that is authentic, ongoing, and based on mutual commitment and shared life intentions. The Home Office assesses this by reviewing the consistency and credibility of the evidence provided.

Key Characteristics: Genuine relationships typically show emotional commitment, shared decision-making, mutual support through difficulties, and concrete plans for a future life together in the UK.

12. Intention to Live Together Permanently in the UK

Applicants must demonstrate a clear and genuine intention to live together permanently in the UK. This includes:

  • Immediate living arrangements following visa approval
  • Long-term residence expectations and plans
  • Employment or study intentions in the UK
  • Integration plans within the UK community

Extended Absences: Extended periods spent outside the UK must be justified and consistent with the intention of permanent UK residence. Frequent or prolonged absences may raise doubts about genuine intentions.

13. Previous Relationship Broken Down Permanently

Where either partner has been previously married or in a civil partnership, evidence must be provided to show that the relationship has permanently ended. Acceptable evidence includes:

  • Decree absolute or final divorce order
  • Dissolution certificate for civil partnerships
  • Death certificate for deceased former partners
  • Legal documentation proving relationship termination

Legal Dissolution Preferred: While legal dissolution is preferred, in limited circumstances, alternative evidence may be accepted. However, this requires careful preparation and strong supporting documentation.

14. UK Unmarried Partner Visa Financial Requirement

Applicants must demonstrate that they can be adequately maintained in the UK without reliance on public funds. This generally requires a minimum gross annual income of £29,000, unless exemptions apply.

Important: The financial requirement must be met at each application stage, including entry clearance, extension, and settlement. Strict evidential standards apply, and failure to meet them is a common reason for refusal.

Income Sources Considered:

  • Employment income (sponsor and/or applicant)
  • Self-employment earnings
  • Non-employment income (rental, dividends, etc.)
  • Cash savings over £16,000
  • Pension income

15. How to Satisfy the UK Unmarried Partner Visa Financial Requirement

The financial requirement may be met through various means or combinations:

Employment Income:

Current employment with same employer for 6+ months, or with different employer earning same amount for 12+ months.

Cash Savings:

Savings must be held for at least 6 months. Formula: (Savings - £16,000) ÷ 2.5 = annual income contribution.

Self-Employment:

Requires tax documents, accounts, and evidence of ongoing business.

Non-Employment Income:

Property rental, dividends, interest, maintenance payments, etc.

Combination Rules: In certain cases, a combination of sources is permitted. Additional considerations apply where the sponsor receives specific benefits or where exceptional circumstances exist.

16. Unmarried Partner Visa Accommodation Requirement

Applicants must provide evidence of suitable accommodation that meets all legal requirements:

  • Legally occupied (owned or rented with permission)
  • Not overcrowded according to UK housing standards
  • Compliant with public health and safety regulations
  • Available without recourse to public funds

Evidence Required: Tenancy agreement, mortgage statement, property inspection report, or letter from property owner confirming accommodation availability.

17. Unmarried Partner Visa English Language Requirement

Applicants must demonstrate English language proficiency unless exempt. Requirements vary by application stage:

Application Stage CEFR Level Required Common Evidence
Initial Application A1 (Beginner) Approved test certificate
Extension A2 (Elementary) Approved test certificate
Settlement (ILR) B1 (Intermediate) Approved test or degree taught in English

Exemptions: Available for applicants aged 65+, those with physical or mental conditions preventing testing, and nationals of majority English-speaking countries.

18. UK Unmarried Partner Visa Supporting Documents Checklists

Applications must be supported by the correct documents in the prescribed format. Essential documents include:

Relationship Documents:

  • Evidence of meeting and relationship duration
  • Communication records and photographs
  • Statements from friends and family
  • Evidence of previous relationships ending

Financial Documents:

  • Bank statements (6-12 months depending on category)
  • Pay slips covering required period
  • Employment letter confirming income
  • Tax documents for self-employed

Accommodation Documents:

  • Tenancy agreement or mortgage statement
  • Property inspection report if required
  • Council tax statement

Identity and Status Documents:

  • Valid passports for both partners
  • Sponsor's proof of status in UK
  • Birth certificates if applicable
  • English language test certificate

Important: Generic checklists should be used cautiously, as each case requires tailored evidence. Missing or incorrect documentation can lead to refusal, delays, or the need for reapplication.

19. UK Unmarried Partner Visa Application Fee

The application fee varies depending on application location:

  • Applications from outside the UK: £1,938
  • Applications from within the UK: £1,321

Additional Charges:

  • Priority Service: £500-£800 (reduces processing time)
  • Super Priority Service: £1,000 (next-day decision where available)
  • Immigration Health Surcharge: £1,035 per year (must be paid upfront for visa duration)

20. UK Unmarried Partner Visa Processing & Decision Waiting Times

Processing times differ based on application location and service level:

Priority Service 30 working days 5 working days
Super Priority Service Not available Next working day

Processing Variables: Priority and Super Priority options may reduce decision times where available, subject to eligibility. Complex cases may take longer regardless of service selected.

21. Duration of a UK Unmarried Partner Visa

Successful applicants are granted leave as follows:

  • Entry clearance (from outside UK): 33 months initial grant
  • In-country applications: 30 months initial grant
  • Extensions: Further 30 months to complete 5-year qualifying period

Settlement Pathway: After completing 5 continuous years on the Unmarried Partner Visa route, applicants become eligible to apply for Indefinite Leave to Remain (settlement).

22. Switching Into the Unmarried Partner Visa Route From Within the UK

Applications may be made from within the UK, provided the applicant holds valid leave exceeding six months in an eligible category:

Eligible Categories for Switching:

  • Skilled Worker Visa
  • Student Visa
  • Graduate Visa
  • Other family route visas
  • Most work visa categories

Visitor Restrictions: Visitors are generally required to apply from overseas. Those in the UK on a Standard Visitor Visa cannot switch to the Unmarried Partner route from within the UK.

23. Working in the UK on an Unmarried Partner Visa

Employment Rights: Unmarried Partner Visa holders are permitted to work in the UK without restriction. There are no limitations on:

  • Type of employment (including self-employment)
  • Number of hours worked
  • Specific employers or sectors
  • Minimum salary requirements (beyond general employment laws)

24. Indefinite Leave to Remain as an Unmarried Partner

After five continuous years on this route, applicants may qualify for settlement, subject to meeting all requirements:

Key Requirements for ILR:

  • Continuous residence in UK for 5 years
  • Relationship remains genuine and subsisting
  • Financial requirement continues to be met
  • English language at B1 level
  • Passed Life in the UK test
  • No serious criminal record or immigration breaches

Continuous Residence: Applicants must not have spent more than 180 days outside the UK in any 12-month period during the 5-year qualifying period. Some discretion may apply in exceptional circumstances.

25. UK Unmarried Partner Visa Frequently Asked Questions

Where Can I Apply for A UK Unmarried Partner Visa?

Applications must be submitted either from the applicant's country of residence or from within the UK if eligible. Visitors must apply from overseas. The application location affects processing times, fees, and specific requirements.

How Do I Apply for a UK Unmarried Partner Visa?

Applications are submitted online using the appropriate form. Supporting documents must be prepared in advance and uploaded in accordance with Home Office procedures. Key steps include:

  1. Complete online application form
  2. Pay application fee and Immigration Health Surcharge
  3. Upload required supporting documents
  4. Book biometrics appointment
  5. Attend appointment and submit biometrics
  6. Wait for decision

When Can I Apply for a British Passport?

Applicants may apply for British citizenship after obtaining indefinite leave to remain and meeting the relevant residence and eligibility requirements. For partners of British citizens, you can apply immediately after receiving ILR. For others, you must wait 12 months after ILR.

What if My Unmarried Partner Visa Application Is Refused?

Refused applications usually carry a right of appeal. Appeals are heard by the First-tier Tribunal and may involve oral or written evidence. Grounds for appeal typically involve human rights considerations. Alternatively, you may be able to reapply addressing the reasons for refusal.

What if I Cannot Satisfy the Requirements for an Unmarried Partner Visa?

Where standard requirements cannot be met, applicants may still be considered under human rights grounds in exceptional circumstances. This requires demonstrating that refusal would result in unjustifiably harsh consequences under Article 8 of the European Convention on Human Rights.

UK Transit Visa Overview

The UK serves as a major international travel hub with extensive global connections, making it a common transit point for travelers worldwide. Understanding when and how to obtain a transit visa is crucial for smooth international travel.

Important: A UK Transit Visa allows you to pass through the UK on your way to another country. The type of transit visa you need depends on whether you'll pass through UK border control and how long you'll be in the country.

What is a Transit Visa?

A transit visa permits you to change flights in a country while travelling onward to another destination. For instance, if you are flying from the USA to Germany and your journey includes a stopover in the UK, you may need a UK transit visa to allow you to pass through the UK. In most cases, a transit visa is only required if you are a national who normally needs a visa for the country where the stopover occurs.

Two Types of UK Transit Visas:

  • Visitor in Transit visa: Required if you need to pass through UK border control
  • Direct Airside Transit visa: Required if you will remain airside and not pass through border control

Note: If you intend to remain in the UK for more than 48 hours, a Transit visa will not be suitable. In this situation, you should apply for a Standard Visitor visa instead. Likewise, if you plan to transit through the UK frequently over a period longer than six months, even for short stopovers, the Standard Visitor visa is the appropriate option.

Do I Need a Transit Visa for the UK?

Not all travellers are required to obtain a Transit visa to pass through the UK briefly. You will generally not need a Transit visa if you hold any of the following:

  • An EU passport (although from April 2025, an Electronic Travel Authorisation will be required for stays of up to 6 months)
  • An EU Settlement Scheme Family Permit
  • A Home Office travel document (such as documentation confirming refugee or stateless status)
  • A Standard Visitor visa
  • A Marriage Visitor visa

Important: If your stay in the UK will exceed 48 hours, a Transit visa is not appropriate and you should apply for a Standard Visitor visa instead.

Types of UK Transit Visa

There are two main categories of UK Transit visas: the Direct Airside Transit visa and the Visitor in Transit visa. Each serves different transit scenarios depending on whether you need to pass through UK border control.

Key Difference:

The main distinction between the two visa types is whether you will pass through UK border control during your transit. Your airline can confirm which process applies to your specific itinerary.

Direct Airside Transit Visa – If You're Not Going Through UK Border Control

This visa is for travellers who are passing through the UK on the way to another country without entering the UK through border control. If you are not exempt from needing a Transit visa and the following apply, you will likely need a Direct Airside Transit visa:

  • You have a connecting flight departing from the UK
  • You will remain airside and will not pass through UK border control
  • You will depart the UK within 48 hours of arrival

Check with your airline: Your airline can confirm whether you will be required to pass through border control during your stopover.

Visitor in Transit Visa – If You're Going Through UK Border Control

This visa is intended for travellers who must pass through UK border control while transiting to another country. If you are not exempt and the circumstances below apply, you will likely need a Visitor in Transit visa:

  • You have a connecting flight from the UK to another destination
  • You are required to pass through UK border control on arrival (for example, to collect and re-check luggage)
  • You will leave the UK within 48 hours of arrival

Advisory: It is advisable to check with your airline to confirm whether border control will be required during your transit.

Stays of Over 48 Hours: Standard Visitor Visa

If you plan to stay in the UK for more than 48 hours, you will not qualify for a Transit visa and must instead apply for a Standard Visitor visa. This visa allows visits of up to six months for various purposes, including tourism, short-term study, visiting friends or family, and limited business activities.

If the UK is one of the destinations you wish to visit and your stay will exceed 48 hours, this visa is likely the correct choice. The same applies if your layover is longer than 48 hours.

The Standard Visitor visa allows activities such as:

  • Tourism and travel within the UK
  • Visiting friends and relatives
  • Volunteering for up to 30 days
  • Transiting through the UK
  • Certain permitted business activities, such as attending meetings, interviews, or conferences
  • Approved paid engagements, such as delivering a short lecture series or performing in a show

Important: If you already hold a valid Standard Visitor visa, you may use it instead of applying for a separate Transit visa, even if the UK is not your final destination.

EU citizens currently do not need a visa to enter the UK for stays of up to six months. However, from April 2025, an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) will be required for visits of six months or less.

Required Documents for UK Transit Visa

When applying for a UK Transit visa, you will need to submit several supporting documents, which typically include:

  • A valid passport
  • Any additional relevant travel documents
  • Proof of permission to enter your destination country (such as a passport, residence permit, green card, or valid visa)
  • Evidence explaining the purpose of your visit to the destination country
  • Proof of onward travel from the UK (such as flight confirmations, tickets, boarding passes, or travel agent documents)

Important: You should also carry these documents with you when travelling, as UK border officials may request to see them on arrival.

Applicants must clearly demonstrate that they intend to leave the UK within 48 hours and that they are authorised to enter their destination country. Failure to provide adequate evidence may result in refusal, so it is recommended to apply only after your onward travel has been confirmed.

How to Apply for UK Transit Visa (Step by Step)

To apply for a UK Transit visa, you must complete several steps.

Application Process:

  1. Submit Online Application: Complete your application through the UK government website
  2. Biometric Appointment: Attend an appointment at a UK visa application centre to provide your photograph and fingerprints
  3. Home Office Review: Your application will be reviewed by the UK Home Office
  4. Decision Notification: You will be informed of the decision by email
  5. Visa Issuance: If approved, you will receive either a physical visa vignette in your passport or access to a digital immigration status

Processing Note: Once issued, the visa may be used to enter the UK for transit purposes.

Application Fee for UK Transit Visa

The fee depends on the type of Transit visa you apply for:

Current Fees (As of 2025):

  • Direct Airside Transit visa: £39 (may vary slightly by country)
  • Visitor in Transit visa: £70 (with potential variations depending on the location of application)

Important: These fees are subject to change. Always verify the current fee on the official UK government website before applying.

UK Transit Visa Processing Time

Most applications receive a decision within three weeks. However, processing times can vary depending on several factors:

  • The accuracy and completeness of your application
  • Whether all required documents are provided
  • The volume of applications being processed
  • Your specific circumstances and nationality

Recommendation: It is recommended to apply well in advance of your travel date and to respond promptly to any requests for additional information from UK Visas & Immigration.
